Who am I?

Hello, my name is Douglas J. Honeycutt, but everyone I know calls me Joey. I'm based out of Jasper, Alabama. I am a husband, a father, all-around cool guy, and I am also kinda good at computering. As a matter of fact, web development, design, and programming have been my hobby and my passion for years. Now I'm seeking to put my skills to work in an environment where they will be rigorously tested by the standards of the professional market.

I cut my teeth on programming back in the late '90s with an old copy of Microsoft QBasic. I really didn't know what I was doing at the time, but making the magic box make pretty colors made me happy. Not long after, I discovered HTML and my parents got me a set of books on web design for Christmas that year. I read the covers off them, and by the late '00s I had a couple of web pages online I'd coded myself and paid out of pocket to host.

Since then, I've come to know and love many other languages and technologies. Python specifically granted me an insight into programming that has allowed me to quickly acclimate to other new environments with rapid progress and absorption. While my focus has since shifted toward more front-end centric languages, Python will always be my baby, my first love, and an invaluable part of my toolkit.

When I'm not working to further my coding skills, I like to spend my time on a number of other hobbies including music, fantasy novels, tabletop RPGs and worldbuilding. You can catch some of my escapades around the internet at the following links:
